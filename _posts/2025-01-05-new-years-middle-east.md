There are few places in the world that do New Year's Eve quite like Dubai. The fireworks over the Burj Khalifa are genuinely one of the most spectacular things I've ever seen — and I've seen a lot of fireworks.

But the real magic of this trip was what came after: crossing the border into Jordan and discovering one of the world's most extraordinary landscapes.

## Dubai: New Year's Eve Done Right

The key to Dubai NYE is positioning. The Burj Khalifa viewing areas fill up hours in advance, but the view from Dubai Creek Harbour is equally spectacular with significantly smaller crowds.

**Where I stayed:** The Rove Downtown — affordable by Dubai standards (around £80/night), perfectly located, and genuinely well-designed. Skip the five-star hotels unless someone else is paying.

**What to eat:** Al Ustad Special Kabab in Deira for the most authentic Iranian food I've had outside Tehran. Queue outside, worth every minute.

## Jordan: Ancient Wonders

Petra needs no introduction, but it deserves one anyway. The Treasury at sunrise, when the light turns the rose-red sandstone to gold, is the kind of moment that makes you understand why people dedicate their lives to travel.

Go on a weekday. Hire a local guide. And for the love of everything, don't just do the Treasury — walk the full Siq, climb to the Monastery, and stay until the crowds thin out in the afternoon.

## Wadi Rum: Sleeping Under Stars

Wadi Rum is Mars on Earth. The landscape is so alien it's been used as a filming location for *The Martian* and *Star Wars*. Spend at least one night in a Bedouin camp — the silence and the stars are worth more than any hotel view.

**Practical note:** Hire a jeep and guide through your camp — the companies at the entrance gate are overpriced and the experience is impersonal.

## Getting Between Dubai and Jordan

Fly Dubai to Amman — flights are around £80-120 return if you book 6+ weeks ahead. From Amman, hire a private driver for the day to Petra (around £60) or join a small group tour.
