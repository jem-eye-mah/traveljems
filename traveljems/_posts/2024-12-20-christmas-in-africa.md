Contrary to popular culture, there is more to Africa in December than partying at Quilox or the Library until 7am, or savoring the special fried rice at Kozo in Ghana.

If you're looking to venture beyond the bustling energy of Lagos' iconic Detty December celebrations or Ghana's vibrant Afrochella and endless nightlife, follow me as we discover more of the history that West Africa has to offer.

## Badagry, Lagos State

Just an hour and a half from Lagos, Badagry is one of Nigeria's most historically significant towns. The beaches are stunning — wide, uncrowded, and fringed with palm trees — but the real draw is the history.

The Badagry Heritage Museum tells the story of the transatlantic slave trade through artefacts and oral history in a way that's both sobering and essential. It's the kind of place that stays with you long after you leave.

## Boti Waterfalls, Ghana

Three hours from Accra, the Boti Waterfalls are one of Ghana's best-kept secrets. The falls cascade over ancient volcanic rock into a clear pool below — and during the rainy season, they're spectacular.

The hike to get there takes about 20 minutes through lush forest, and on weekdays you'll practically have the place to yourself. Pack a picnic, wear good shoes, and thank me later.

## Where to Stay

For Accra I'd recommend staying in Cantonments or Labone — both safe, walkable neighbourhoods with great restaurants and easy access to the rest of the city.

In Lagos, Victoria Island or Lekki Phase 1 are your best bets. Avoid anywhere that adds significant traffic to your journey — Lagos traffic is a full travel experience in itself.

## Final Thoughts

West Africa in December is genuinely magical. Yes, the parties are incredible — I'm not telling you to skip Afronation. But carving out even two or three days for history, nature, and slower travel will make your trip infinitely richer.
